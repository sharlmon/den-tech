What To Do Immediately After Spilling Water on Your MacBook

The first 30 minutes are critical. Here's the exact steps to take to maximize your chances of saving your MacBook from liquid damage.

Quick Steps:

  1. Power off immediately — Don't try to save your work. Hold the power button.
  2. Unplug everything — Charger, USB devices, headphones.
  3. Flip it upside down — Let gravity pull water away from the logic board.
  4. Do NOT use rice — It doesn't work and leaves starch residue.
  5. Do NOT use a hair dryer — Heat can warp components.
  6. Bring it to us within 24 hours — Corrosion starts immediately.

7 Tips to Extend Your MacBook's Lifespan

Simple habits that can add years to your MacBook's life and save you thousands in repairs.

Key Tips:

  • 🔋 Keep battery between 20-80% when possible
  • 🌡️ Use on hard, flat surfaces for proper ventilation
  • 🧹 Clean the keyboard regularly with compressed air
  • 🔄 Keep macOS and apps updated
  • 💾 Back up regularly with Time Machine
  • 🛡️ Use a quality case for transport
  • ⚡ Use the original Apple charger

Can Your Data Be Recovered From a Dead MacBook?

Your MacBook won't turn on, but your photos and documents might still be intact. Here's what's possible.

Recovery Chances:

  • ✅ Logic board failure — Very high recovery rate
  • ✅ Liquid damage (early) — Usually recoverable
  • ⚠️ SSD controller failure — Often recoverable
  • ⚠️ Encrypted T2/M1+ drives — Depends on chip integrity
  • ❌ Physical SSD damage — Very difficult

MacBook Not Charging? Here's Why

Before you panic, try these diagnostics. Not all charging issues require a repair — but some definitely do.

Common Causes:

  • 🔌 Faulty cable or adapter (try a different one)
  • 🧹 Dirty USB-C port (clean gently with toothpick)
  • 🔋 Battery management (reset SMC/PRAM)
  • ⚡ Damaged charging circuit (needs repair)
  • 🔥 Liquid damage to power rail (needs repair)
